**Transparent Tuesday: How I'm Helping Foreigners Navigate Japan's Utility Setup with a Step-by-Step Guide**

Hey fellow indie hackers,

As a solo builder, I've been working on a side project that I'm sharing with you all today. If you've ever lived in Japan as a foreigner, you know how overwhelming it can be to set up basic utilities like electricity, gas, water, and internet. That's why I created a free online tool to help simplify the process: [Japan Utility Setup](https://humancronadmin.github.io/japan-utility-setup/).

**The Problem**

Moving to a new country can be daunting, and setting up utilities is often a major hurdle. As a foreigner in Japan, you may not speak the language fluently, and navigating the complex process of setting up utilities can be frustrating. That's why I saw an opportunity to create a resource that would make it easier for people to get settled in Japan.

**The Solution**

My tool provides a step-by-step guide for setting up electricity, gas, water, and internet in Japan. It's designed to be easy to follow, even if you don't speak Japanese. The guide includes:

* A checklist of required documents and information
* A step-by-step walkthrough of the setup process for each utility
* Tips and recommendations for choosing the best providers
* Links to additional resources and support

**Why it matters**

I believe that everyone deserves to have access to basic utilities, regardless of their language proficiency or cultural background. By providing a clear and concise guide, I hope to make it easier for foreigners to get settled in Japan and start building their lives.

**The Tech**

The tool is built using simple HTML, CSS, and JavaScript, and is hosted on GitHub Pages. I chose this approach to keep costs low and make it easy to maintain and update the content.
